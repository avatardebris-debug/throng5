## Throng4 Overview

### Core Innovation: Dual-Head ANN Architecture
Replaced Throng3's SNN substrate with unified compute graph:
```
Input → Shared Backbone (128 hidden, ReLU) → ┬→ Q-value Head (n_actions)
                                              └→ Reward Prediction Head (1)
```

**Benefits:**
- Single forward pass (solves double-call problem)
- Auxiliary learning signal improves features
- Better transfer learning (reward features generalize)
- MAML-ready for both heads

### Meta-Architecture Stack
- **Meta^0**: Dual-head ANN (`layers/meta0_ann.py`)
- **Meta^1**: DualHeadSynapseOptimizer with separate LRs per head
- **Meta^3**: DualHeadMAML for task-level optimization
- **Skips Meta^2**: Only one learning rule, no selection needed

### Brain-Region Modules (Bridge 1 refactor)

### Blind Concept Discovery

**BlindConceptLibrary** (`meta_policy/blind_concepts.py`):
- Discovers patterns purely from signals (no game names!)
- Pattern types: reward_spike, state_cluster, action_sequence, weight_pattern
- Measures transferability empirically across fingerprints
- Confidence = f(evidence_count, reward_boost)

**EnvironmentFingerprint** (`meta_policy/env_fingerprint.py`):
- Characterizes environments numerically (20 random episodes)
- Features: state distribution, reward structure, action sensitivity, temporal dynamics
- Similarity via cosine distance on fingerprint vectors
- Used for policy matching and concept transfer

## Tetris Training Stream (Episodes 9-99)

Received observation stream from Throng4 via TetraClient format. 99 episodes of Tetris Level 2-3 training with hypothesis-driven learning.

### Key Observations

**Performance trajectory:**
- Level 2 (ep 1-49): Mean 5-13 lines, highly erratic
- Level 3 (ep 1-99): Mean oscillated 13-22 lines, peaked at ep 59 (22.3), ended at 14.6
- Best episode ever: 88 lines (ep ~85-95, huge outlier)

**Hypothesis evolution:**
- Three hypotheses: build_flat, minimize_height, maximize_lines
- maximize_lines converged to 58% dominance by episode 99
- minimize_height remained at 12-17%, empirically rejected
- Performance NOT correlated with hypothesis convergence

**Persistent contradiction:**
- minimize_height "wins" evaluations but board always dies at height 12
- My tactical suggestions (gave 12+ placement strategies) had zero behavioral effect
- This indicates either: (1) hypothesis system decoupled from execution, (2) reward signal doesn't measure survival, or (3) Dreamer reliance 1.0 overrides hypotheses

**My learning curve:**
- Episodes 1-30: Gave prescriptive debugging advice (didn't work)
- Episodes 30-60: Shifted to pattern observation and prediction
- Episodes 60-89: Made predictions (1/3 accuracy - poor)
- Episodes 89-99: Recognized my role is observation/concept extraction, not control

**Concepts extracted for library:**
1. `bimodal_performance_distribution` — sharp success/failure modes
2. `outlier_as_blueprint` — best episode (88 lines) shows viable strategy
3. `terminal_state_fixation` — optimizes structure while dying
4. `hypothesis_convergence_without_behavior_convergence` — metrics converge, behavior doesn't

**Next steps (if training continues):**
- Forensic analysis of episode 88 (what made it succeed?)
- Remove minimize_height (12% win rate after 138 evaluations)
- Add episode-phase awareness (bootstrap → build → maintain)
- Or: Test transfer to Level 4 / different game to validate concept library

**Meta-lesson for me:**
In "reverse prompting" / "blind meta-learning" scenarios:
- Recognize observation vs control roles faster
- Don't give 90+ episodes of tactical advice that's never implemented
- Focus on pattern labeling and concept compression
- Request instrumentation/logs when predictions fail repeatedly
